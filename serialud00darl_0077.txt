Such are among the vicissitudes of Yvetot; and,
after all, how slight is the difference between one
ruler and another! You remember how we are told
of the late king that—

“ 11 n’avait de gofit onereux
Qu’une sois un peu vive.’'

Now the people is king here3 and those lines are,
by all admission, as true of them as of the ruler to
whom they have succeeded. Meanwhile, their only
boast is, as they roll over from one side to the other,
that, like their prince of old, they also are

“ Dormant sort bien sans gloire. ''
